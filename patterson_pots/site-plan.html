Overview

Purpose

Our purpose is to promote these amazing pieces of handmade artwork so that the crafter of these pots can continue to make art that inspires and intrigues everyone that sees them. The goal is to break even and to not profit much, so the pots are also pretty inexpensive.

Audience

The audience that we have is specifically people that are interested in custom made, hand crafted pottery. It's not a big audience, but it's for people that want brilliant pieces of artwork for a relatively low price.

About the Creator

About the author, Ken Patterson. My connection to pottery. All my life I have been fascinated with making pottery on a wheel. It just looked like fun, but I never had an opportunity to do it, until I visited my first duty on active duty in the Armyâ€”Ft Polk Louisiana, now called Ft Johnson. The military installations are geared towards keeping the troops happy, and that means keeping them occupied in their off-duty hours. One of the big facets of such activity is the system of recreation facilities on military installations. Ft Polk had a collection of pottery wheels and kilns, and some people to help the troops learn. I jumped right in and quickly became fascinated with the process. I didn not have many opportunities at other installations because each seems to have a different focus, whether auto mechanics, wood working, lapidary, photography, or whatever. However, after we moved to Twin Falls I signed up for pottery classes at CSI. Then I was hooked. I took the class 3 times, each with a different instructor, so I learned different approaches and became motivated to get involved. I found some older used equipment, a wheel and a kiln, in the community, which I bought and cleared out a space for in my small shop. Over the years I have upgraded my equipment. I now have two wheels and a nice electric kiln. I had a gas kiln but decided the electric was easier to use. Now I am retired from dental practice, so I spend about 15-20 hours per week in my studio. It is relaxing and I enjoy creating esthetic and useful pieces. I enjoy the breadth of what pottery includes. I once wondered how someone could get a Masters of Fine Arts in pottery and ceramics. Now I know that pottery and ceramics is a field that is absolutely open for anybody who wants to do it, and for whatever reason they choose.
